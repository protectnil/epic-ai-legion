# Epic AI® Trademark Policy

## Background

protectNIL Inc. is committed to the broad adoption of Epic AI® IVA Core as an open-source platform. Ensuring proper use of the Epic AI® mark is essential to maintaining the clear association between the mark and the quality, security, and reliability for which Epic AI® products are known. This document establishes the policies governing use of Epic AI® trademarks by others.

## Relationship to the Apache License 2.0

The Apache License 2.0 governs your rights to use, reproduce, modify, and distribute the source code of this project. Section 6 of the Apache License 2.0 explicitly states:

> "This License does not grant permission to use the trade names, trademarks, service marks, or product names of the Licensor, except as required for reasonable and customary use in describing the origin of the Work."

This trademark policy governs only the use of the Epic AI® name, logos, and associated marks. Nothing in this policy modifies, limits, or extends the code rights granted under the Apache License 2.0.

## Nominative Fair Use

To accurately identify Epic AI® products, it may be necessary to refer to them by name. The basic rules for nominative fair use are: be accurate and not misleading, use only the word mark (not logos), and do not use the mark more prominently or frequently than necessary.

Examples of permitted nominative fair uses:

- "You can download the Epic AI® IVA Core source code under the Apache License 2.0 at https://github.com/protectnil/epic-ai-core."
- "Our product is compatible with @epicai/core."
- "I recommend Epic AI® IVA Core for federated enterprise orchestration."

Use of the Epic AI® name in any nominative context does not imply endorsement, sponsorship, or affiliation with protectNIL Inc.

## Using Epic AI® Trademarks in Product Branding

You may not use Epic AI® trademarks in your own product name. You may use the phrase "Compatible With Epic AI® IVA Core" in product descriptions, provided you also follow the rest of this policy. Use of "Powered By," "for the Epic AI® platform," or similar conventions that imply a closer relationship requires prior written permission from protectNIL Inc.

## Derivative Works

The Apache License 2.0 allows you to create forks and derivative works of Epic AI® IVA Core. However, you must follow these trademark guidelines:

- **Use a different name.** You may not call your derivative work "Epic AI" or any confusingly similar name. You may refer to it only as "a derivative work of," "derived from," or "compatible with" Epic AI® IVA Core.
- **Remove all branding.** Remove the Epic AI® logo, hexagon device, and any associated trade dress from your distribution.
- **Provide clear attribution.** State that your version is modified and not endorsed by protectNIL Inc.

## Logos and Visual Marks

The Epic AI® logo and hexagon device are not licensed under the Apache License 2.0. You may not use, reproduce, or create derivative works of Epic AI® logos, or adopt confusingly similar visual marks, without written permission from protectNIL Inc.

## Domain Names and Social Media

You may not use Epic AI® trademarks in domain names, social media account names, or handles without written permission from protectNIL Inc. Use of trademarks in domain names is not considered nominative fair use because the domain name refers to your offerings, not merely to our products.

## Prohibited Uses

The following uses violate this policy:

- Branding products or services in a manner confusingly similar to Epic AI® branding
- Providing software or services consisting of Epic AI® software under alternative names that incorporate the Epic AI® mark
- Using company or product names that may be associated in users' minds with Epic AI® or protectNIL Inc.
- Claiming protectNIL Inc. approval or endorsement without a written agreement

If you become aware of potential misuse of Epic AI® marks, please contact **legal@protectnil.com**. If we contact you about your use of our marks, we appreciate prompt corrective action — and if you have questions about our requests, we welcome the opportunity to address them.
